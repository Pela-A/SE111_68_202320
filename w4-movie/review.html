REVIEW

Alexander Pela
January 30, 2023

Cars holds a special place in my heart due to it being my favorite childhood movie. When this movie released on DVD one of my cousins parents had bought it. So one day I went over to hangout and we watched the movie. I was a 5 year old boy who thought cars as a whole were cool. I liked the movie a ton because it had cars and Mater was funny.

I recently rewatched the move and like with a lot of Pixar and Disneys movies between 2000-2010 the movie ages like fine wine. From a surface level its just a movie about cars and racing however Pixar always manages to add a bunch of really cool life lessons or themes which allows the movies to be digested by a younger and older audience at the same time. They briefly talk about how modernization affects small towns and businesses, how its important to do the right thing for others, and that sometimes you have to think outside of the box to be in the top 1% of the world.

I feel like most of Disney and Pixars movies today lack that dynamic and that is why most people only remember the first movie and not the other two movies which abandoned those themes and life lessons. This is a movie that I plan to show my kids one day just for the memories and because I like it so much.
